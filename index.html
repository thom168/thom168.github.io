[ca] [Hilbert] $R$ Noetherian $\implies R[t]$ Notherian.
fix $\I\ideal R[t]$. so $\I$ is a subset of $R[t]$ closed under addition, scaling from $R$, and multiplication by $t$. thus $I_d=\{r\in R\mid \exists a_dt^d+\dots+a_0\in\I \st r=a_d\}$ is an increasing chain of ideals of $R$. for each $d$ up to stabilization, fix $I_d=\gen{r_{jd}}$, and fix $f_{jd}\in \I$ of degree $d$ whose leading coefficients is $r_{jd}$. ergo $\I=\gen{f_{jd}}$ is a finitely generated ideal of $R[t]$. ▨
[an] theorem. $f\in L^1(\mathbf{R})$, $\hat{f}\equiv0\implies f\equiv0$ ae.
[Newman] for $a\in\R$ we have $\disp\int_{-\infty}^{a}f(x)e^{-2\pi i(x-a)\xi}\text{d} x= \int_{\infty}^{a}f(x)e^{-2\pi i(x-a)\xi}\text{d} x$. the lhs may be extended holomorphically to the upper half plane, and the rhs to the lower half plane. the whole $F_{a}(\xi): \C\to\C$ is continuous, and thus holomorphic. however $|F_{a}(\xi)|\le\left\Vert f\right\Vert_1$ , so by Liouville $F_{a}$ is constant. finally, by dominated convergence $0=\disp\lim_{s\to -\infty}F_a(is)=F_a(0)=\int_{-\infty}^a f$. since $a\in\R$ was arbitrary, $f\equiv0$ ae. ▨
